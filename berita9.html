Comparatives and Superlatives

What Are Comparatives?

Comparatives are used to compare two things. For short adjectives, add -er. For longer adjectives, use more.

  • tall → taller
  • interesting → more interesting

Examples:

  • This book is more exciting than the last one.
  • My car is faster than yours.

What Are Superlatives?

Superlatives show the highest degree of a quality, comparing three or more things. Use -est for short adjectives and most for longer adjectives.

  • fast → the fastest
  • beautiful → the most beautiful

Examples:

  • She is the smartest student in the class.
  • This is the most delicious cake I’ve ever had.

Spelling Rules

Rule Example
Adjectives ending in -y → change “y” to “i” happy → happier → happiest
One-syllable, consonant-vowel-consonant → double final consonant big → bigger → biggest

Irregular Forms

Adjective Comparative Superlative
good better best
bad worse worst
far farther / further farthest / furthest

Using Comparatives and Superlatives

  • Comparative: This movie is longer than the last one.
  • Superlative: That was the most exciting match of the season.
  • Comparative: Today is hotter than yesterday.
  • Superlative: He is the best player in the team.
