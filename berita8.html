Common English Expressions

Everyday Expressions You Should Know

English has many expressions that are commonly used in daily conversation. These phrases often have meanings different from their literal words.

Popular Expressions and Their Meanings

Expression Literal Meaning Actual Meaning Example Situation
How are you? Asking about your health or condition A polite greeting to ask how someone is doing Meeting a friend or colleague
What’s up? What is above? A casual way to ask “What’s going on?” or “How are you?” Talking to friends informally
Long time no see It has been a long time since I saw you A friendly greeting when meeting someone after a long period Meeting an old friend after months or years
Break a leg Wishing someone to actually break a leg A way to say “Good luck” especially before a performance Before a stage play or presentation
Piece of cake A slice of cake Something very easy to do Talking about a simple task or test

Informal and Formal Variations

Some expressions have formal alternatives. For example:

  • How are you? (formal) vs. What's up? (informal)
  • Good luck (formal) vs. Break a leg (informal)

It's important to choose expressions depending on the situation and the people you are speaking with.
