Modal Verbs

What are Modal Verbs?

Modal verbs are auxiliary verbs that express ability, possibility, permission, obligation, advice, or prediction. They do not change form according to the subject and are followed by the base form of the main verb.

Common Modal Verbs

  • Can
  • Could
  • May
  • Might
  • Must
  • Shall
  • Should
  • Will
  • Would

Functions and Examples

Modal Verb Function Example Sentence
Can Ability, permission I can swim. / Can I use your phone?
Could Past ability, polite permission, possibility She could run fast when she was young. / Could I leave early?
May Permission, possibility You may leave now. / It may rain later.
Might Possibility (less certain) He might come to the party.
Must Obligation, strong certainty You must finish your homework. / He must be the new teacher.
Shall Suggestions, offers (formal) Shall we go for a walk?
Should Advice, expectation You should see a doctor.
Will Future actions, willingness I will help you tomorrow.
Would Polite requests, hypothetical situations Would you like some coffee?

Difference Between Modal Verbs and Auxiliary Verbs

Auxiliary verbs (be, have, do) help form tenses, questions, and negatives, and they change form based on subject and tense.

Modal verbs express modality (possibility, permission, etc.) and do not change form based on subject or tense. Also, modal verbs are always followed by the base verb without "to".

Example:

  • Auxiliary: She is running.
  • Modal: She can run fast.
