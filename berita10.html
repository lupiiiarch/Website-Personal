Prepositions

What Are Prepositions?

Prepositions are words that show the relationship between a noun (or pronoun) and other words in a sentence. They often indicate direction, place, time, or introduce an object.

Types of Prepositions

  • Prepositions of Time: at, on, in, since, for
  • Prepositions of Place: at, in, on, over, under, next to
  • Prepositions of Direction/Movement: to, into, onto, out of, toward, through

Common Prepositions and Example Sentences

Preposition Type Example
at Time / Place I’ll meet you at 5 p.m. / He is at the door.
on Time / Place My birthday is on Monday. / The book is on the table.
in Time / Place She was born in July. / The keys are in my bag.
into Direction He walked into the room.
through Direction The train passed through the tunnel.

Common Prepositional Phrases

  • In front of: The car is parked in front of the house.
  • Next to: She sat next to her friend.
  • Out of: He jumped out of the car.
  • According to: According to the news, it will rain tomorrow.
  • Because of: The game was canceled because of the storm.

Common Preposition Mistakes

  • ❌ He is married with her. → ✅ He is married to her.
  • ❌ She is good in math. → ✅ She is good at math.
  • ❌ I arrived to the airport. → ✅ I arrived at the airport.
  • ❌ They discussed about the problem. → ✅ They discussed the problem.
