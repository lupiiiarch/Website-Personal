Tenses: Present, Past, and Future

What is Tense?

Tense indicates the time when an action happens. It shows whether the action is in the present, past, or future.

Present Tense

Simple Present

Used for habitual actions, general truths, and facts.

  • Example: She works every day.

Present Continuous

Used for actions happening right now or temporary situations.

  • Example: They are studying English at the moment.

Present Perfect

Used for actions that happened at an unspecified time before now or started in the past and continue to present.

  • Example: I have visited London.

Past Tense

Simple Past

Used for actions completed in the past at a specific time.

  • Example: She visited her friend yesterday.

Past Continuous

Used for actions that were happening at a certain time in the past.

  • Example: They were watching a movie when I called.

Past Perfect

Used to describe an action completed before another past action.

  • Example: He had finished dinner before she arrived.

Future Tense

Simple Future (Will)

Used for spontaneous decisions, promises, or predictions.

  • Example: I will help you with your homework.

Going to (Future Plan)

Used for plans or intentions and predictions based on present evidence.

  • Example: She is going to travel next week.

Summary Table of Tenses

Tense Usage Example

Present Continuous Actions happening now They are studying English.
Present Perfect Past actions with present relevance I have visited London.
