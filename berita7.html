Phrasal Verbs

What are Phrasal Verbs?

Phrasal verbs are combinations of a verb and one or more particles (prepositions or adverbs) that create a meaning different from the original verb.

They are very common in everyday English and often have idiomatic meanings.

Common Phrasal Verbs and Their Meanings

Phrasal Verb Meaning Example Sentence
Look after Take care of She looks after her younger brother.
Get up Wake up and rise from bed I get up at 7 a.m. every day.
Give up Stop trying, quit He gave up smoking last year.
Take off Remove (clothes); leave the ground (plane) Please take off your shoes. / The plane took off on time.
Put on Wear (clothes, accessories) She put on her jacket before going out.

Using Phrasal Verbs in Daily Conversations

Phrasal verbs are widely used in informal and formal speech. Knowing them helps you understand native speakers better and sound more natural.

Try to learn phrasal verbs in context rather than just memorizing them alone.

Tips for Remembering and Differentiating Similar Phrasal Verbs

  • Group phrasal verbs by particle (e.g., all with up, off, out) to see patterns.
  • Practice making sentences to understand the meaning in context.
  • Use flashcards or apps focusing on phrasal verbs.
  • Pay attention to differences in meaning, e.g., give up vs give in.
