Subject-Verb Agreement

Basic Rules of Subject-Verb Agreement

Subject-verb agreement means the subject and verb in a sentence must agree in number — both must be singular or both must be plural.

  • If the subject is singular, the verb must be singular.
  • If the subject is plural, the verb must be plural.

Example:

  • She walks to school every day. (singular subject and singular verb)
  • They walk to school every day. (plural subject and plural verb)

Singular vs Plural Subjects

Subjects can be singular or plural, and this determines the verb form.

Subject Verb Example
Singular Verb + s/es The dog barks loudly.
Plural Verb base form The dogs bark loudly.

Special Cases

Collective Nouns

Collective nouns refer to a group but can take singular or plural verbs depending on whether the group acts as one unit or as individuals.

  • Singular verb: The team is winning the match.
  • Plural verb: The team are arguing among themselves.

Indefinite Pronouns

Some indefinite pronouns are always singular, some always plural, and some can be both.

Indefinite Pronoun Verb Form Example
Everyone, someone, nobody Singular Everyone is welcome.
Few, many, several Plural Many are invited.
All, some Singular or Plural All of the cake is gone. / All of the cookies are gone.

Examples and Exceptions

  • The news is surprising. (news is singular)
  • Mathematics is difficult. (treated as singular)
  • The scissors are on the table. (plural meaning)
  • Either the teacher or the students are late.

Tips to Avoid Mistakes

  • Identify the subject carefully before choosing the verb.
  • Ignore words between the subject and verb (such as "along with", "as well as").
  • Be careful with collective nouns—consider if the group acts as one or many.
  • Remember indefinite pronouns usually take singular verbs, except some that are plural.
  • Practice reading and listening to correct English to develop a natural sense.
