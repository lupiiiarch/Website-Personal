Common Mistakes in English

Introduction

Making mistakes is part of learning. However, understanding common mistakes in grammar and vocabulary can help you avoid them and speak English more confidently.

Common Grammar and Vocabulary Mistakes

Mistake Correction Explanation
I have visited Paris last year. I visited Paris last year. “Last year” refers to a specific time in the past, so use past simple, not present perfect.
She can to drive. She can drive. Modal verbs are followed by the base form of the verb, without “to”.
He don’t like pizza. He doesn’t like pizza. Use “doesn’t” with third-person singular (he, she, it).
I’m boring. I’m bored. “Bored” describes how you feel. “Boring” describes something that causes boredom.
She said me the news. She told me the news. Use “tell” with an object; use “say” without one. Example: She told me / She said it.

Why Do These Mistakes Happen?

  • Interference from native language structure.
  • Misunderstanding grammar rules.
  • Learning from incorrect sources (e.g. informal social media).
  • Limited vocabulary or improper verb usage.

Tips to Avoid Mistakes

  • Read and listen to authentic English content regularly.
  • Review grammar basics often and take notes.
  • Ask native speakers or teachers to correct you.
  • Use grammar apps or tools to practice daily.
  • Don’t be afraid to make mistakes — learn from them!

Effective Learning Suggestions

  • Keep a personal list of mistakes and corrections.
  • Practice writing sentences using corrected grammar.
  • Join English speaking groups or conversation clubs.
  • Take online quizzes to reinforce your learning.
